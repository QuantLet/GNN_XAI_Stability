Name of Quantlet: GNN_XAI_Stability

Published in: Expert Systems with Applications (submitted)

Description: Do Graph Embeddings Stabilize SHAP? A Controlled Decomposition
             under Distribution Shift. Implements a per-feature SHAP
             attribution stability diagnostic --- Jensen-Shannon divergence
             across random and temporal validation regimes with
             block-bootstrap intervals, a paired block-permutation
             procedure, Benjamini-Hochberg adjustment, and an explicit
             null-resampling baseline (~35% rejection at nominal alpha=0.05).
             Decomposes the headline gain through nested controls:
             diagonal-scale random/permuted/noise-injected controls
             reproduce most of the gain via column augmentation; a
             stricter covariance-matched control absorbs the remainder on
             XGBoost, with the cross-pair flagged fraction falling below
             the null baseline. Conclusion: column augmentation plus
             matched embedding covariance account for the gain; no
             graph-information channel beyond covariance is detectable by
             this diagnostic. Validated on IEEE-CIS Fraud Detection with
             heterogeneous GraphSAGE embeddings and replicated on the
             Elliptic Bitcoin transaction dataset (diagonal-scale
             controls only).

Keywords:    XAI, SHAP attribution stability, distribution shift,

             control, fraud detection, IEEE-CIS, Elliptic, block bootstrap,
             BH-FDR
